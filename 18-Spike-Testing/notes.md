# Apache JMeter - Spike Testing

## What is Spike Testing?

Spike Testing evaluates an application's behavior during a sudden increase or decrease in user traffic.

The goal is to verify system stability and recovery after unexpected traffic spikes.

---

## Objectives

- Simulate sudden traffic surges
- Measure response times
- Identify temporary failures
- Evaluate recovery after the spike

---

## Common Scenarios

- Flash Sales
- Ticket Booking
- Banking Salary Credit Days
- Product Launches
- Examination Results

---

## Important Metrics

- Response Time
- Throughput
- Error Percentage
- Recovery Time
- Server Resource Usage

---

## Best Practices

- Use realistic spike patterns.
- Monitor CPU, Memory, and Network usage.
- Analyze response time before, during, and after the spike.
- Avoid View Results Tree during high-load execution.

---

## Interview Questions

### What is Spike Testing?

Spike Testing evaluates application behavior when traffic suddenly increases or decreases.

---

### Why perform Spike Testing?

To verify that the application can handle sudden traffic surges and recover once the load returns to normal.

---

### How is Spike Testing different from Stress Testing?

Stress Testing gradually increases load to identify the breaking point.

Spike Testing introduces an immediate surge in traffic and evaluates system recovery.

---

## Summary

Spike Testing ensures an application remains stable during sudden traffic spikes and recovers quickly once the load decreases.
